This project is the final Capstone of the Google Advanced Data Analytics Certificate. It simulates a real-world consulting scenario for Cyclistic, a fictional bike-sharing company in Chicago, to uncover data-driven insights that inform business decisions and strategic marketing efforts.

Key Features:

  1. Business Problem Definition:
    The primary goal was to understand the behavioral differences between casual riders and annual members and identify data-backed strategies to convert casual riders into subscribers.
  2. Robust Data Cleaning & Preparation:
    12 months of ride data were wrangled using Pandas, including handling nulls, outliers, and inconsistent formatting across over 5.6 million rows.
  3. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA):
    Applied descriptive statistics, correlation matrices, and visual analytics to extract trends in ride duration, frequency, day-of-week usage, and station patterns using Seaborn and Matplotlib.
  4. Key Insights:
    Casual riders were more likely to ride on weekends, use docked bikes, and have shorter trip durationsâ€”insights that shaped targeted marketing recommendations.
  5. Actionable Recommendations:
    Suggested strategies include incentivized loyalty programs, targeted weekday promotions, and app-based nudging to convert casual users to members.
  6. Presentation & Storytelling:
    Delivered results through a Jupyter Notebook and professional visual dashboard, translating technical findings into actionable business language for non-technical stakeholders.
